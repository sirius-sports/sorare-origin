Sorare MLB Strategy Guide
Lineup, Scoring & Market Tips
Sorare MLB translates real game stats directly into scores. That means who you pick, which slot you put them in, and when you buy matters enormously. Here are four practical strategies covering lineup building, score maximization, market tactics, and mistakes to avoid.

STRATEGY 01

GW Lineup Building (7-Card System)

Your lineup has 7 slots: Starting Pitcher (SP), Relief Pitcher (RP), Corner Infielder (CI), Middle Infielder (MI), Outfielder (OF), Hitter Flex, and All-Player Flex. Whether you put a pitcher or hitter in that last Flex slot is the key strategic decision.

Prioritize Teams with More Games in the GW
A GW typically spans 3–4 days. Players on teams with more scheduled games get more at-bats (hitters) and more appearance opportunities (especially RPs). Why? Scoring is cumulative — more games = more chances to rack up points.
Slot an "RP-Registered Starter" in the RP Position
Some pitchers who actually start games are registered as RPs in Sorare's system. Using them in your RP slot is a major edge. Why? Normal RPs throw 1–2 innings. A de-facto starter throws 5–6 innings, earning far more IP points and easily completing "10 outs" daily missions.
You can have at most 6 players from the same team in one lineup. You cannot fill all 7 slots with one team.
STRATEGY 02

Maximizing Your Score

Target Home Run Hitters for Explosive Scores
A home run is worth +10 points. Add RBIs (+3 each) and runs scored (+3), and a grand slam can yield +25 points in one swing. Caveat: Power hitters tend to strike out more (-1 each). Check batting average and OBP alongside HR numbers.
Two-Way Players (e.g., Shohei Ohtani) Need Specific Slots
Two-way cards can be used in SP, CI, or Hitter Flex slots, but cannot be placed in the All-Player Flex slot. Why? The Flex slot can't determine whether to count pitching or batting stats, so two-way cards require a role-specific slot.
Grind Hot Streak (PvE) for Steady Essence Rewards
Clear the target score to earn Essence. Since 2026, skipping a GW no longer resets your win streak. Strategy: Skip weeks where your cards won't perform well, and go all-in on favorable matchups.
STRATEGY 03

Card Buying & Market Strategy

Cards That Hold Value
• In-Season cards: Current-year cards get a +5% bonus and access to exclusive, higher-reward competitions.
• High-K, high-IP pitchers: Consistent scorers whose market value stays stable.
Best Time to Buy: Late Season to Off-Season
Prices spike during the season on hype, then stagnate in the off-season even for promising players. Tip: Avoid buying during Launch Boost periods at season start when prices are inflated. Buy during quiet periods.
Snipe Reward Cards via Direct Offers
Cards with a trophy icon were earned as rewards. Owners often have less attachment to them, making them 5–10% cheaper than market rate via direct offers. Tip: Modest discounts (e.g., offering $10 for a $12 card) have higher acceptance rates.
STRATEGY 04

Common Beginner Mistakes

Not Checking the Injured List (IL)
Injured players score 0 and their card value can crash. Always check IL status before building your lineup or buying a card.
Overpaying at Season Start
Buying during Launch Boost hype often leads to losses when prices correct. Check recent sales history on SorareData before purchasing.
Buying Based on Floor Price Alone
The cheapest listing isn't always market value — it could be an old-season card or a non-scoring player. Check the price chart over the past few months before buying.
