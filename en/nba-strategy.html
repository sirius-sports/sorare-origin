Sorare NBA Strategy Guide
Lineup, Points Cap & Market Tips
Sorare NBA converts real NBA stats into scores in real time. Thanks to the Points Cap system, simply stacking the best cards doesn't guarantee wins. Here are four practical strategies covering lineup building, score optimization, market tactics, and mistakes to avoid.

STRATEGY 01

GW Lineup Building (5-Card & Points Cap)

Your lineup consists of 5 cards with no position restrictions — any player in any slot. However, each competition has a Points Cap (maximum total L10 for all 5 players), and your combined L10 must fit within it.

Understand L10 (Cost)
Each player's cost is their average score across their last 10 games played. DNPs (games not played) are excluded — only actual appearances count. Rookies and players without game time start at L10 = 0.
Why it matters: L10 is a simple moving average, meaning sudden role changes (e.g., a backup becoming a starter due to injury) take several weeks to reflect. This lag is where top managers find their edge.
The Spacer Strategy (L10 = 0 Hack)
Intentionally include one player with L10 of 0–1 who won't play. This costs only 1 cap point, letting you load up the other 4 slots with superstars (L10 of 50+). The spacer scores 0, but the firepower of your remaining 4 players more than compensates.
Best used: In GWs where mid-tier players (L10 = 20–25) have low expected value. Maximizes overall team ceiling.
Contender vs. Champion
Contender: No MVP system — all 5 players' L10 counts toward the cap. Easier to earn rewards; great for building your collection.
Champion: The player with the highest L10 becomes MVP and is excluded from the cap. Lets you lock in one superstar. Higher-rarity card rewards.
GWs run Mon–Thu and Fri–Sun (twice per week). Lineups lock 10 minutes before each GW starts.
STRATEGY 02

Maximizing Your Score

Sorare NBA scoring is action-based, directly tied to real NBA stats. Understanding the point values helps you pick the right players.

Know the Scoring Breakdown
Points +1.0, Rebounds +1.2, Assists +1.5, Blocks +3.0, Steals +3.0, Turnovers -2.0. Each 3PT made adds +1.0 bonus (total +4.0 per three). Double-double +1.0, Triple-double +2.0 (cumulative).
Key insight: Blocks and steals at +3.0 each are disproportionately valuable. All-around defenders who also grab rebounds and dish assists offer the best L10-to-score ratio.
Prioritize Volatility for Top Finishes
A player who consistently scores 30 is good, but a 3PT-heavy shooter who can explode for 50+ is better for podium finishes. Each three is worth +4.0 total — that's your ceiling booster.
Why? Top placements come down to fractions of a point. High-ceiling players give you the best shot at winning.
Multi-Game GWs Are Your Best Friend
Sorare NBA uses only the best single-game score from each player during the GW. Players with 2–3 games get multiple shots at a great performance, making them far safer picks. Always check game count within the GW.
Exception: For the Champion MVP slot, a one-game superstar like Luka Doncic can still be worth it if his ceiling is unmatched.
Stack Your Bonuses
Rarity: Limited +0%, Rare +10%, Super Rare +20%, Unique +40%.
XP Level: +0.5% per level (max Lv20 = +10%).
In-Season card: +5%.
Collection bonus: Same team + same season cards = up to +5%.
Why it matters: Rarity + XP + Season + Collection can add up to +15%. In a game decided by tenths of a point, this is the difference between winning and losing.
STRATEGY 03

Card Buying & Market Strategy

Cards are traded via Auctions (primary market) and Manager Sales (secondary market). Understanding In-Season vs. Classic and L10 dynamics is key to growing your portfolio.

In-Season vs. Classic Cards
In-Season: +5% season bonus, access to exclusive high-reward tournaments with smaller player pools — higher chance of placing.
Classic: No bonus but retains XP. Use for Essence grinding (PvE), then craft In-Season cards with the earned Essence.
Budget play: Enter every GW with Classic cards to grind Essence, then craft In-Season cards for free.
Exploit the L10 Lag (Arbitrage)
When a star gets injured, their backup suddenly starts — but their L10 stays near 0 for weeks. Track injuries via ESPN depth charts and Rotowire and scoop up low-L10 breakout candidates before the market catches on.
Essence & Sealing Cycle
Seal or scrap unwanted cards to convert them into Essence. Use Essence to craft new card packs. Recycling old-season cards into next season's In-Season cards is the core asset loop.
Buy During the Off-Season
Market activity drops and prices fall in the off-season. Stock up on next season's key players at discounted prices. Injured stars can also be long-term value buys.
Note: Wait 1–2 weeks after an injury for the price to bottom out before buying.
Team Stacking for Collection Bonus
Concentrating cards from one team (e.g., Cavaliers) gives all those players a permanent +5% collection bonus. Combined with XP (+10%) and rarity bonuses, this yields 15%+ stacking — a decisive edge in tight competitions.
STRATEGY 04

Common Beginner Mistakes

NBA-specific pitfalls that cost new managers dearly. Knowing these in advance saves you from unnecessary losses.

Not Checking the Injury Report
A DNP player costs you their full L10 in cap space but scores 0. Check injury reports before every GW via Sorare NBA Jet or Rotowire.
Classic scenario: "Giannis ruled out last minute — 0 points, dropped from contention to bottom tier."
Ignoring Game Count in the GW
A player with 1 game vs. 3 games has vastly different upside. Low-cost bench players with more games = more chances to get playing time and break out. Always check the schedule.
Overlooking Transaction Fees
Manager Sales charge 3% for credit/debit cards. PayPal is 4.25% (USD) or 3.25% (EUR/GBP). For frequent traders, ETH wallet transactions can be more cost-effective (gas fees vary).
Frequent Trading Destroys XP
Selling or trading a card halves its XP. High-level cards earn bigger score bonuses and sell for more. Don't flip cards you've invested XP into unless you have a strong reason.
Over-Diversifying on a Small Budget
With limited funds, focus on Contender tournaments first to earn rewards and flip cards for profit. Keep spare cards so L10 fluctuations don't push you over the cap. Start with Limited cards — they're the most liquid for beginners.
Progression: Win Contender rewards → flip cards for profit → gradually enter Champion.
