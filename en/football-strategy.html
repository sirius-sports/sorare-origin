Sorare Football Strategy Guide
Lineup, Scoring & Market Tips
The key to consistent wins in Sorare Football isn't flashy goals — it's fielding players who are guaranteed to start, in the right slots, with the right captain. Here are four actionable strategies covering lineup building, score optimization, market tactics, and common mistakes to avoid.

STRATEGY 01

GW Lineup & Player Selection

Your lineup consists of 5 cards: GK, DF, MF, FW, and Extra. The #1 rule is simple: never include a player who won't play (DNP = 0 points). Even one absent player tanks your entire team's chances.

Use L5 / L10 Appearance Rate to Pick Starters
Check each player's last 5 (L5) and last 10 (L10) appearance rate and prioritize players close to 100%. Target an average score of 55+. Why? In Sorare, scores only count when a player actually plays. Consistency beats explosive potential every time.
Wait Until the Last Minute to Lock In
You can swap players right up until each match's kickoff (flexible deadline). Check official predicted lineups and SorareInside's starting XI info before finalizing. Tip: Don't lock in early. Optimizing based on last-minute lineup announcements and injury news is what separates winners.
Pick an Explosive FW / Attacking MF as Captain
The captain gets a +20% to +50% bonus depending on the competition. The go-to pick is a key striker who's guaranteed at least 60 points (Level 1) with a single goal.
STRATEGY 02

Maximizing Your Score

Defensive Stack: Pair GK + Same-Club DF
Run a GK and DF from the same team. If they keep a clean sheet, the DF gets +10 points and the GK's score jumps significantly. Target: Matches against weaker opponents or defensively solid teams where a clean sheet is likely.
Aim for the DF Double-Double (+4 pts)
Intercepts, successful tackles, and net duels won — hit 2+ in any two categories for +4 points. All three categories = +6. Hit 3+ in each = +12. Tip: Defensive-minded DFs facing stronger attacking teams actually have more chances to rack up these stats.
Put a Consistent MF in the Extra Slot
Deep-lying playmakers from possession-heavy clubs consistently score 50–60 points through pass accuracy alone (AAS), even without goals. Note: Substitutes now start at 35 points (unified base), making "super subs" more viable than ever.
Big clubs (Man City, Real Madrid, etc.) rotate heavily due to deep squads. Mix in mid-table starters who are nailed-on regulars for more consistency.
STRATEGY 03

Card Buying & Market Strategy

Invest in U23 Starters
Dedicated U23 leagues mean young regular starters have sustained demand and hold their value well.
Buy Before a Step-Up Transfer
Players moving from smaller leagues (J-League, MLS, Eredivisie) to top-5 European leagues see card values spike after the transfer. Buy before the move is announced. Edge: Leagues with less English-language coverage (like J-League) offer information asymmetry advantages.
Buy in the Off-Season, Sell Before Kickoff
Market interest drops during the off-season, pushing prices down. Prices peak just before the season starts or after a hot streak. Buy low, sell high is the fundamental cycle.
Know When to Use In-Season vs. Classic Cards
Cash rewards require the latest In-Season cards, but they're expensive. For building a strong team on a budget or grinding Essence (PvE), Classic cards are the smart play.
STRATEGY 04

Common Beginner Mistakes

Listing a Card on the Market During a GW
If you list a card that's in an active lineup, it's removed from your team and scores 0. Always wait until scores are finalized before listing.
Overpaying in Manager Sales
Manager Sales (user-set prices) can be above market value. Beginners should start with New Card Auctions (official listings) to get fair prices.
Ignoring Relegation & Transfer Risk
If a player's club is relegated to an uncovered division, the card shows NG (Not Game-eligible) and scores 0. The card isn't lost — it reactivates if the club is promoted or the player transfers. Avoid long-term holds on relegation-zone players.
Going All-In on One Star Player
Spending your entire budget on one elite card and filling the rest with bench warmers is a recipe for disaster. Prioritize 5 guaranteed starters over 1 superstar + 4 question marks.
